Metaphor 3: The Cosmic Seed

How did the structures of the universe, like galaxies, originate? Cosmic inflation says that in the first fraction of a second, the universe expanded exponentially. This stretched microscopic quantum fluctuations from the "fizz" into macroscopic seeds of varying density. The slightly denser regions, born from a random quantum event, became the gravitational anchors for the cosmic web we observe today.
